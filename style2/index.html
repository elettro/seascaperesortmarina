Seascape Resort & Marina

Owner Presentation. Recommended Website Style Direction.

This report recommends a lighter, more minimal, highly professional visual system for Seascape Resort & Marina. The goal is simple. Increase booking confidence, raise perceived value, and present the property as a calm, established waterfront destination in Marathon rather than a bright tourist-style Keys brand.

Recommended Positioning

Seascape should look like a private waterfront resort with quiet confidence. Light backgrounds should lead the experience. Deep navy should anchor it. Reviews should appear lower on the homepage to help close the booking decision after the guest has seen the rooms, property, and marina experience.

  • Light luxury presentation
  • Minimal but warm visual language
  • Professional booking-first homepage flow
  • Typography that feels established and premium
Executive Summary

What should change to improve booking presentation

The strongest direction for Seascape is not darker, louder, or more tropical. It is cleaner, lighter, calmer, and more refined. Let the photography provide the Keys energy. Let the design provide trust, clarity, and value perception.

Background Direction

Use warm ivory, soft sand, and white as primary page backgrounds. This makes the site feel more current, more premium, and easier to scan.

Anchor Color

Use deep navy as the brand anchor for headers, buttons, footer, navigation, and elegant accents instead of heavy full-page dark sections.

Typography

Use Cormorant Garamond for headlines and brand expression. Use Inter for body copy, navigation, forms, and booking-path clarity.

Homepage Conversion

Place guest reviews lower on the homepage after the experience and room sections. This helps reduce hesitation at the exact point the guest is deciding.

Homepage Booking Logic

Recommended homepage flow to improve trust and conversion

The homepage should move the guest through a calm but direct decision path. Show the feeling first. Then show the property strengths. Then use reviews as closing proof.

Recommended Homepage Order

  1. 1

    Hero Section

    Strong waterfront imagery, clear positioning, direct-booking call to action, and immediate trust statement.

  2. 2

    Rooms Preview

    Show room types fast. Let users compare visually. Add direct actions to view details or check availability.

  3. 3

    Experience Section

    Sell the lifestyle of Seascape. Calm water. Poolside feel. Marina access. Quiet Marathon atmosphere.

  4. 4

    Marina Differentiator

    Use this as a competitive advantage. It separates Seascape from standard Keys lodging.

  5. 5

    Guest Reviews

    Place reviews lower on the homepage where they support the final booking decision rather than interrupt the top story.

  6. 6

    Location and Map

    Reinforce Marathon positioning and convenience once the guest already wants the stay.

  7. 7

    Final Booking Call

    End with one clean booking section. No clutter. No competing messages.

Why Reviews Matter Lower on the Page

Reviews are strongest when they confirm what the guest already saw. They work best after the visitor has absorbed the look, rooms, and waterfront experience. At that point, reviews reduce friction and add confidence.

Review focus 1

Waterfront setting, calm atmosphere, and the unique marina feel.

Review focus 2

Clean rooms, relaxing property, and memorable Marathon location.

Review focus 3

Return visits, hidden-gem language, and direct-booking confidence.

Final Recommendation

The visual target for Seascape

Seascape should feel like a calm, lightly elevated, professionally presented waterfront resort in Marathon. The site should not look bright-tourist, dark-heavy, or overly nautical. It should feel light, minimal, warm, and booking-ready. That is the strongest route to higher perceived value and stronger direct-booking presentation.
