Execution order

What Elettro and Dean bring to the table.

This page is less about pricing experiments and more about the specific production, marketing, automation, creative, and revenue-support work that can be built for Seascape Marina and Atlantic Bay.

Foundation Layer

1. Website Production

Get the website up to code with the latest and greatest, built with custom HTML and CSS through AI-assisted production and cleaned up for a stronger modern presentation.

  • Build or refine a custom site with a clean luxury Keys feel instead of a standard template look.
  • Set up and optimize relevant integrations between the website, Google Search Console, Google Ads, Google Business Profile, and analytics systems.
  • Create cleaner funnel paths for rooms, excursions, weddings, and full-property inquiries.
  • Use AI production to accelerate content blocks, layouts, sections, and future landing pages.
Search + AI + Access

2. Optimize for Search, AI, and Handicapped Folks

Make the site more visible, more readable, and more usable. This is a core production service, not an afterthought.

  • Improve page structure, headings, metadata, internal linking, and local search targeting.
  • Optimize for AI search with better semantic structure, answer-based content, FAQs, and schema support.
  • Improve readability, contrast, alt text, button labels, and general accessibility support.
  • Build a stronger technical base for future ranking and future AI visibility.
Phase Two

3. Add Smart Integrations After the Basics Are Live

Once the main website foundation is solid, expand into tools and custom development layers that support the property long term.

  • Start with manually curated local events and local recommendations.
  • Later consider building a Keys event scraper or event aggregation tool.
  • Add better operational integrations only after the core website and search systems are stable.
  • Expand carefully so the site grows in function without getting bloated.
Tracking

4. Phone Tracking, Call Tracking, Email Tracking, and CRM Upgrades

Get visibility into what is producing inquiries and where stronger systems may be needed over time.

  • Track calls and call sources so the property knows what content and campaigns are driving action.
  • Track email collection and lead sources more intentionally.
  • Review current CRM or inquiry handling and recommend light upgrades if needed.
  • Build a cleaner reporting trail across website, calls, forms, and future campaigns.
Occupancy Support

5. Email Marketing for Last-Minute Open Rooms

Organize and proactively collect emails so the properties can directly promote short-notice openings and staycation opportunities.

  • Build an email capture system into the website and other guest touchpoints.
  • Create campaigns for last-minute room openings and South Florida drive-down staycations.
  • Use email as a practical occupancy tool, not only a newsletter.
  • Keep building the owned audience instead of depending only on third-party booking channels.
Content Production

6. YouTube and Video Content Marketing

Produce visual marketing content that sells the properties, the Keys lifestyle, the excursions, and the atmosphere.

  • Create YouTube videos and shorts around rooms, marina life, sunsets, fishing, snorkeling, and destination energy.
  • Support YouTube growth with selective ad spend if desired.
  • Repurpose clips across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts.
  • Use content production as both a branding engine and a search engine asset.
Merchandising

7. Print-on-Demand Clothing and Merchandise

Offer Seascape and Atlantic Bay merchandise through Stashbox.ai with low overhead and strong flexibility.

  • Create Seascape merch and Atlantic Bay merch for online sale.
  • Use QR codes and website links to push guests into the shop.
  • Use apparel and accessories as both brand extension and guest souvenir products.
  • Keep inventory light through print-on-demand production.
In-Room Sales

8. In-Room QR Code Sheets

Use one-sheet QR sales and information pages in a high-attention location. Guests stare at the back-of-door area every day.

  • Design clean one-sheets for merch, coffee, excursions, future stays, and useful property info.
  • Use QR codes for Captain Kevin and relevant excursion partners.
  • Keep them visually strong and easy to scan from a distance.
  • Turn a dead space into an active sales and service surface.
Product Opportunity

9. Private Label Coffee from Don Pablo Coffee

You already have a working relationship with Don Pablo Coffee in Miami and can bring world-class private label coffee to the properties and the online shop.

  • Create Seascape and Atlantic Bay branded coffee products.
  • Evaluate how coffee is currently offered to room guests and build around that system, including possible K-cups or other room-ready formats.
  • Sell it in rooms, online, and through QR code pathways.
  • Use it as a strong physical product tied directly to the guest experience.
Brand Differentiator

10. Keys Music, Playlists, and Music SEO

Dean can produce Keys music that sets the tone on the website, expands brand presence across streaming platforms, and gives the resorts an identity most competitors do not have.

  • Create original chill Keys music tied to Seascape, Atlantic Bay, Marathon, Tavernier, excursions, and waterfront life.
  • Distribute it across 25+ major music sites for additional search and branding exposure.
  • Use playlists on the website to set tone and mood without overpowering the booking experience.
  • Guide listeners from the artist ecosystem back toward the brand and the websites.
Reach Expansion

11. Influencer Collaboration Opportunities

There are influencer relationships available that can be used to create additional visibility, content, and social proof for the properties.

  • Host strategic stays and create content partnerships.
  • Focus on travel, lifestyle, ocean, music, and experience-driven creators.
  • Use influencers as part of content creation, not only reach.
  • Align the right influencer with the right property vibe.
Unique Upsell

12. Guest Pre-Order Entertainment

Let guests pre-order entertainment in a simple way, similar to how a mini-bar concept presents optional add-ons, except for curated live experiences.

  • Offer future-scheduled options like a DJ, a band, or poolside live entertainment.
  • Make the request process easy through the website, inquiry forms, or in-room QR paths.
  • Position it as a premium customization option rather than a last-minute operational burden.
  • Create a memorable differentiator most small hotels do not offer.
Experience Layer

13. Organize Excursions with Captain Kevin and Others

Excursions are part of the guest value, part of the content engine, and part of the upsell system.

  • Feature Captain Kevin prominently while evaluating other relevant partners.
  • Create clear pages, QR links, and inquiry paths for fishing and snorkeling bookings.
  • Bundle excursions into room-driven packages over time.
  • Use excursions as recurring content fuel for video and social growth.
High-Value Funnel

14. Wedding Packages and Full Property Lockout Presentation

Improve the representation of weddings on location and build a stronger presentation funnel around intimate Keys weddings and full-group buyouts.

  • Create stronger wedding presentation pages, visuals, and messaging for both properties.
  • Organize wedding package options from intimate events to larger multi-day stays.
  • Present a full property lockout offer where one group can take over the entire property.
  • Build a dedicated inquiry funnel around private resort experiences, wedding weekends, and group events.
Long-Term Thought

15. Private Country Club Style Membership Concept

This remains an early thought, but there may be a future opportunity for a high-deposit, high-annual-fee membership model built around preferred access and repeat use.

  • Explore a private club style concept instead of a traditional timeshare approach.
  • Position it around preferred access, status, and recurring relationships.
  • Use it as a future recurring revenue layer if it aligns with owner goals and inventory realities.
  • Protect owner control while testing whether a membership lane makes sense for small boutique properties.

Property fit

Why this fits Seascape and Atlantic Bay.

These are boutique waterfront properties. That means the visual identity, music identity, excursion identity, and wedding identity matter more than they would at a generic larger hotel. Smaller can feel more private, more curated, and more memorable.

  • Seascape can lean into marina energy and adventure.
  • Atlantic Bay can lean into calm Keys relaxation and intimate stays.
  • Both can benefit from shared systems and shared production support.
